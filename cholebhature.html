Chole Bhature

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (chole)
  • 2 tea bags or 1 tsp tea leaves (for boiling chickpeas)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • For Bhature:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp semolina (sooji)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed to knead dough)
  • Oil for deep frying

Procedure

  1. Soak chickpeas overnight. Pressure cook with tea bags and salt until soft. Discard tea bags.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds, then onions. Sauté until golden.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 2 minutes. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates.
  4. Add all spices and mix well. Add boiled chickpeas and simmer for 15–20 minutes.
  5. Garnish with coriander leaves.
  6. For Bhature: Mix flour, semolina, baking powder, salt, and yogurt. Knead into a soft dough using water.
  7. Cover and rest for 2 hours. Divide into balls and roll into circles.
  8. Deep fry in hot oil until golden and puffed.
  9. Serve hot bhature with spicy chole and sliced onions or pickle.
